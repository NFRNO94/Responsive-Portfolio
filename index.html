Who Am I?

My name is Dante Proto. I was born December 6th, 1994 in Mesa, AZ. I currently live in Queen Creek, AZ, where I grew up. In my younger years, I attended St. Timothy's Catholic Academy 1st through 6th grade. I went to a public school for the first time in the 7th grade at Payne Jr. High. It was around this time when I started playing my favorite sport, American Football. After football season, I became a wrestler following in my father's footsteps. My Jr. High sports career prepared me for High School level competition. I had a very successful Freshman football season. I moved up to the Junior Varsity level, which ended up being where my football career ended due to illness, and injury. I did wrestle all 4 years of high school, although I was injury prone my first 3 years in highschool. I was contending with Mono as well, which also ruined my Junior Varsity football career. I did have a very good varsity comeback my senior year, and barely missed my chance at going to the state wrestling tournament(by 3 points). After highschool, I wanted to be a mechanical engineer due to inspiration from my neighbor. I quickly realized that calculus was a big part of engineering, and it was my least favorite class in college. I switced my major to General Business after my first year of college. I have been working towards becoming an accountant. I have not obtained a degree in business yet. However, I did study part time for 3 years while working fast food at Panda Express, and later working retail at Costco. I was unable to promote to a full time position at Costco, however, I did realize I enjoyed learning about new technology. I was a sales representative in the electronics section before I left Costco to drive Uber and Lyft full time. It was fun for awhile, however, the hours are hard, and the wear and tear on your vehicle really adds up over time. I landed a job with Dish Network as a Customer Service representative. I handled customer service calls until I was later promoted to technical support. I then handled a mix of tech and customer service calls. As delightful as phone calls may be, I decided that they were not for me, and I was thinking about quitting Dish before Chat Support came to our site. I was a part of the first 2 chat teams that were created at the Phoenix location. I now handle a mix of tech and customer service chats, up to two at a time. I enjoy chat support a lot more than phone calls, as it seems to be more efficient, and I can be more descriptive to help the customer to the best of my ability. The only issue with chat is there is not a lot of room to grow, and promote. That is why I am currently attending UA Coding Bootcamp, to learn about writing code, and using code in real life applications. I also would love to advance to a point where I can make websites, and applications of my own. I hope to find a new career with Dish, or a better opportunity with my new coding knowledge, pending the completion of the Bootcamp. I am excited and eager to learn as much as I can about code!
